What is Sine?

As a function, sine is useful to describe any part of the real world that repeats.
To find the length of the opposite side, sine can be multiplied by hypotenuse.
y = hypotenuse * sin(angle)

Sine is a mistranslation.

Before sine was mistranslated from Sanskrit to Latin, it was called "half-chord."
The chord of a bow is a great visual mnemonic for sine.
The bow is the brown arc, and it's chord is the white line.
We only use half the chord because it is the length of the opposite side.
The hypotenuse would be the length of the chord when drawn.
The bow's chord when drawn is the dotted white lines.
